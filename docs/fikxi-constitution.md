# Fikxi Constitution

Version: 1.0

---

# Our Mission

To make finding trusted local services as easy as ordering food online while creating more earning opportunities for skilled workers.

---

# Our Vision

To become India's most trusted multilingual local services platform where every skilled worker can access fair work and every customer can access trusted help.

---

# Core Principles

## 1. Worker First

Every feature must improve the life of workers.

If a feature helps customers but harms workers unfairly, we will redesign it.

---

## 2. Trust Before Growth

No fake workers.

No fake jobs.

No fake reviews.

Trust is more important than rapid growth.

---

## 3. Simplicity Wins

Our users are not software engineers.

Every screen should be understandable by someone's parents.

---

## 4. Local First

Fikxi is designed for Indian cities.

Every decision should consider local languages, local habits and local workers.

---

## 5. Multilingual by Design

English is never the only language.

Every important feature should support:

- English
- Hindi
- Marathi

---

## 6. Fair Marketplace

Workers should compete using:

- quality
- trust
- availability

—not by continuously lowering prices.

---

## 7. Technology Should Feel Invisible

Users should think:

"This app is easy."

They should never think:

"This app is complicated."

---

## 8. Security is Mandatory

User data belongs to users.

Security is never optional.

---

## 9. Design for Low-End Phones

Fikxi should work smoothly on inexpensive Android devices with slow internet.

---

## 10. Build for 10 Million Users

Even while building the MVP, we should make decisions that won't need to be rewritten completely as the platform grows.

---

# Engineering Principles

Every piece of code should be:

- Readable
- Tested
- Documented
- Reusable
- Secure

---

# Product Philosophy

We are not building another services marketplace.

We are building digital infrastructure for India's skilled workforce.

---

# Founder Promise

Every feature should answer one question:

"Does this genuinely make life easier for customers and workers?"
